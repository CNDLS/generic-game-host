Start by scripting a conversation between you and your students. Everything you add will refer back to that.

Here are some of the things you can specify about your game:

  • a Title,
  • what Winning looks like,
  • the Scenes where the game takes place,
  • Scoreboards, Clocks, and other display Widgets,
  • Resources the player can find and use,
  • and, most importantly, the Rounds that make up the game...

In the framework code, each Round has 3 actors, or 'Dealers.' Each type of Dealer can exchange information in a different way with the player. Together, they form a cycle of communication between your game and your students.

  • Prompter

    I pose a question or propose an idea to start the Round.

  • Listener

    I collect whatever Answer(s) the player makes in response to the Prompt, and I evaluate them.

  • Responder

    I generate any kind of Feedback you specify that fits the player's Answer(s).

Only one Round can be active at a time. Initially, your Rounds will probably look like an exchange of texts. As your game design develops, you may choose to represent information in a variety of other ways (e.g.; video, animations, simulations, data displays, and others).
